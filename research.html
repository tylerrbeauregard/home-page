Deadline: February 15, 2021
  • Description: The theme of the 2021 MSRI-UP is “Parking Functions: Choose your own adventure" and the research leader is Dr. Pamela E. Harris, Associate Professor of Mathematics at Williams College. Parking functions are combinatorial objects defined as follows: Let ℕ := {1, 2, 3, ...} and for n ∈ ℕ define [n] := {1, 2, ..., n}. Consider n parking spaces numbered linearly from 1 to n on a one-way street. Suppose there are n cars, denoted c1, c2, ... cn with parking preferences p1, p2, ... pn respectively. Car ci drives down the one-way street and if it finds pi (its preferred parking spot) unoccupied, it parks there; if it is taken, it would attempt to park in the next available spot along the one-way street. A parking function of length n is an n-tuple in ℝn consisting of the parking preferences that allow all n cars following the aforementioned method. Motivated by the numerous generalizations of this family of combinatorial objects, this research project will focus on the study of generalized parking functions via algebraic, combinatoric, and probabilistic methods.
    Note: Precedence given somewhat to under-represented groups
